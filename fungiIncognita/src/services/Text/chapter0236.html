Stinking Puffball



Lycoperdon foetidum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Shaped like a rounded spinning top, it differs when young from Common Puffball in being covered with pale brown, short spines; groups of which converge like wigwam poles. These later fall off leaving a mesh-like pattern on a brown back-ground. Initially firm and white inside it becomes olive-brown as the spores mature and are released through a small apical pore. It has an unpleasant odour when young.


SIZE 2-5 cm diameter, 2-4 cm high.

HABITAT Frequent in acid woodlands and heaths.

SEASON Summer to autumn.

INEDIBLE Unpleasant smell is offputting.

SIMILAR SPECIES Spiny Puffball has longer spines and a darker spore mass. See Common Puffball.
