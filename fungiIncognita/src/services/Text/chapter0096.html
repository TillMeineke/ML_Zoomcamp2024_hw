Small Brown Milk Cap



Lactarius tabidus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The orange-brown cap soon flattens but usually has a central pimple and is often wrinkled near the middle. The shortly decurrent, yellow-brown, crowded gills release only small amounts of white milk when damaged. This dries yellow on a handkerchief. The stem narrows from its darker-coloured base to the apex.


SIZE Cap 2-4 cm, stem 2-5 cm x 4-8 mm.

HABITAT With broad-leaved trees and most common under birch in wet places, often among Sphagnum (bog) moss.

SEASON Midsummer to autumn.

INEDIBLE Insubstantial with a slightly acrid taste.

SIMILAR SPECIES The darker Sweet Milk Cap produces more plentiful milk which does not dry yellow.
