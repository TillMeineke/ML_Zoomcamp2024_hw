Claviceps purpurea

Autumnal inspection of grass flower heads may reveal a small, brown-black, hard, banana-shaped object. This Ergot sclerotium (resting stage) falls to the ground and produces tiny, drumstick-shaped fruitbodies in spring (see inset). These bear sticky spores which infect grasses (and cereals) via their stigmas and take over the flower to produce a sclerotium. The cause of ergotism from contaminated flour.


SIZE Sclerotium varies with host: 5-20 mm x 2-4 mm.

HABITAT On the flowers of grasses and cereals.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

POISONOUS Used medicinally to prevent haemorrhage after childbirth and to ease migraine.

SIMILAR SPECIES None.
