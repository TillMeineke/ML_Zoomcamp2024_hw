Stinking Parasol



Lepiota cristata



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The small bell-shaped cap matures flat but with a raised red-brown centre surrounded by rings of small, similar-coloured scales on a pinky-white ground. The very crowded, free white gills age browner. The slender silky white stem bears a small transient ring. Unpleasant smell of rubber or tar.


SIZE Cap 2-5 cm, stem 3-6 cm x 3-4 mm.

HABITAT In lawns and pastures and among woodland litter.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

POISONOUS Many closely related species are very poisonous so do not eat small ‘Parasols’.

SIMILAR SPECIES Less common species include those with white, olive or dark-brown cap-scales and those with a fruity smell. Some are very poisonous.
