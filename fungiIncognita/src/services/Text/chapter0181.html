Oyster Mushroom



Pleurotus ostreatus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Increasingly cultivated, its flat, moist, slate-blue or oyster-coloured caps are fan-shaped with an inrolled margin. They dry grey-buff or cream. Decurrent crowded, white to pale straw gills radiate from the point of attachment where they may fork. Small lateral stem, downy when young.


SIZE Cap 5-18 cm, stem 2-3 cm x 10-20 mm.

HABITAT In overlapping clusters on living trunks, dead stumps and logs of broad-leaved trees; rare on conifers.

SEASON Late autumn and through the winter.

EDIBLE Good flavour and firm-fleshed. Cut out stem area.

SIMILAR SPECIES P. pulmonarius is less substantial. See next two pages for species which can be confused with Oyster Mushroom.
