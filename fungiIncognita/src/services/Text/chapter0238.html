Stump Puffball



Lycoperdon pyriforme



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The only puffball which grows in large clusters on rotting wood (This includes dead roots, when it appears to grow on soil). Pear-shaped, and often attached by white strands, it starts granular and creamy-white but matures smooth and ochre-brown when a small hole opens at the apex. When young the inside is firm and white; the upper area turns olive-brown as the spores mature.


SIZE 1-4 cm diameter, 1-6 cm high.

HABITAT Clustered on rotting wood of both broad-leaved and coniferous trees. Very common.

SEASON Summer to late autumn. Old ones persist.

EDIBLE Can be eaten young but has an unpleasant odour.

SIMILAR SPECIES None. Our other puffballs grow on soil.
