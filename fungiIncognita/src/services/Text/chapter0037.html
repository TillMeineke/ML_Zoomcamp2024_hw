Fairy Ring Champignon



Marasmius oreades



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This causes rings of dead grass in lawns. The small, tan, bell-shaped caps retain an umbo in old specimens which dry dirty-white. The wavy margins are often grooved when wet. The adnexed, cream gills are well spaced but have intermediates near the edge. The straw-coloured stem is tough and pliable. It smells of new-mown hay.


SIZE Cap 2-5 cm, stem 3-6 cm x 2-4 mm.

HABITAT Among short grass in lawns.

SEASON Early summer to late autumn.

EDIBLE Use dried in soups and stews. Beware confusion with poisonous species (see below).

SIMILAR SPECIES Often found growing with the poisonous Ivory Clitocybe which lacks the grooved cap edge, has decurrent gills, a brittle stem and a mealy smell.
