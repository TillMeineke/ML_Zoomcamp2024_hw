Larch Bolete



Suillus grevillei



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Species of Suillus are distinguished from those of Boletus or Leccinum by their slimy caps and larger pore size. Larch Bolete (was Boletus elegans) has a slimy, golden-yellow cap which dries glossy. The pores are large, angular and initially covered by a pale yellow veil which forms a ring on the stem. This drops off, leaving a paler area above which the yellow stem is scurfy.


SIZE Cap 4-12 cm, stem 5-10 cm x 12-18 mm.

HABITAT Under or close to larch, often in rings.

SEASON Summer to late autumn.

EDIBLE Remove slimy cap skin. Best in soups.

SIMILAR SPECIES S. aeruginascens has a wrinkled, grey-yellow cap; S. tridentinus has compound orange pores. Slippery Jack is brown and grows under pine.
