Silver Leaf Fungus



Chondrostereum purpureum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This species occurs as a flat crust which turns up to produce small, leathery bracket-like caps. These have faintly zoned, hairy, pale grey upper surfaces with pale undulating margins. When young the underside, like the crust region, is smooth or finely wrinkled and a striking pink-violet colour, fading to dark brown. Previously called Stereum purpureum.


SIZE Patches to 15 cm across, brackets 2-4 cm across.

HABITAT Tiered on dead wood of broad-leaved trees. On living wood of rowan and fruit trees where it causes ‘silver leaf’.

SEASON Throughout the year.

INEDIBLE Very tough.

SIMILAR SPECIES Violet Fir Bracket has pores and grows on conifers.
