Horse Mushroom



Agaricus arvensis



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A large fleshy species smelling of aniseed or bitter almonds. The deeply domed young white cap matures flat. Mostly smooth but may break up into scales at the margin. Older caps have an ochre tinge and flush dull yellow-ochre when bruised. The free grey gills mature dark-brown. The white stem also bruises yellow-ochre. The swollen base does not yellow when cut. It has a large two-layered ring, the lower part star-shaped.


SIZE Cap 5-20 cm, stem 5-15 cm x 15-20 mm.

HABITAT Permanent pastures and road verges; often in rings.

SEASON Early summer to late autumn.

EDIBLE Excellent flavour and texture but see below.

SIMILAR SPECIES See the poisonous Yellow Staining Mushroom.
