Spring Agaric



Agrocybe praecox



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The small to medium-sized cap is broadly convex and a pale tan colour which soon fades to creamy-beige. A cream-coloured veil protects the gills on young specimens and then leaves remains at the cap margin and as a ring at the stem apex on older ones. The adnexed, crowded, cream-coloured gills mature dingy-brown. The stem is paler that the cap and has a bulbous base.


SIZE Cap 3-6 cm, stem 7-9 cm x 6-12 mm.

HABITAT In grass often in open woods and under hedges.

SEASON Spring to summer. Occasionally in autumn.

EDIBLE Must be well cooked; not recommended.

SIMILAR SPECIES Can be confused with Field Mushroom. which has a fleshier cap and free gills which start pink but age very dark brown.
