Cultivated Mushroom



Agaricus bisporus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The shop mushroom is uncommon out of cultivation but too well-known to ignore. It is very similar to Field Mushroom but the mature caps are typically darker due to a covering of brown, fibre-like scales. The crowded free gills are pink in young specimens; very dark brown in old flat caps. The stem bears a more permanent, large white, shaggy ring. Unlike Field Mushroom, the cut flesh reddens slightly.


SIZE Cap 3-10 cm, stem 3-6 cm x 10-15 mm.

HABITAT In rich soil, dung heaps and mushroom compost.

SEASON Summer to autumn.

EDIBLE Can be eaten raw. Older ones have more flavour.

SIMILAR SPECIES The brown-capped variety is similar to some of the Wood Mushrooms (Bleeding Brown Mushroom).
