Honey Fungus



Armillaria mellea



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A destructive species, it kills a wide range of trees and shrubs and spreads to new hosts by bootlace-like strands. The deeply convex caps mature flat with an inrolled margin. The colour ranges from honey-yellow to red-brown. The gills are adnate to decurrent, crowded, flesh-coloured and later have rusty spots. The stem is long with a yellow-white ring; its brown base not swollen.


SIZE Cap 5-15 cm, stem 6-12 cm x 10-20 mm.

HABITAT Densely clustered on both living trunks and dead stumps and roots of broad-leaved trees and conifers.

SEASON Late summer to early winter.

EDIBLE Not easily digested. Must be well cooked.

SIMILAR SPECIES Bulbous Honey Fungus.
