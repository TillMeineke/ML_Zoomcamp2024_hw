Shaggy Earth Tongue



Trichoglossum hirsutum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The dark brown to black fruitbody is not much bigger than a matchstick. It is club-shaped with a smooth, flattened, tongue-like fertile head region which narrows abruptly to a more cylindrical, velvety stem.


SIZE 3-8 cm tall.

HABITAT Among moss in wet acid grassland and especially with bog moss (Sphagnum).

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

INEDIBLE Insubstantial.

SIMILAR SPECIES On sandy soils the Smooth Earth Tongue (Geoglossum cookeianum) is smaller, with a less distinct head region and smooth stem. The rare Green Earth Tongue (Microglossum viride) grows in moss under trees.
