This retains the date-brown colour which on most older caps dries pale buff. It has cream-coloured, crowded adnexed gills. The stem tapers from a much thicker base where it has fine white hairs.


SIZE Cap 4-8 cm, stem 5-9 cm x 10 mm (average).

HABITAT Among broad-leaved tree litter and coniferous needle litter, often in troops and rings.

SEASON Early autumn to early winter.

EDIBLE Not worthwhile.

SIMILAR SPECIES The uncommon C.distorta is red-brown, lacks the greasy feel and swollen stem base. Restricted to coniferous woods.
