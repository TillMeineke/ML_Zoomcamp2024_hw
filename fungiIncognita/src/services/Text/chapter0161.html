Yellow-cracked Bolete



Boletus subtomentosus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This species, often placed in the genus Xerocomus has a pale olive-yellow to light brown, dry cap with a velvety feel. At maturity the surface cracks to reveal the pale yellow flesh. The golden-yellow, angular pores are larger close to the stem and age olive-yellow. It blues faintly when bruised. The creamy-yellow stem often bends and narrows near its base.


SIZE Cap 3-10 cm, stem 4-10 cm x 10-20 mm.

HABITAT Under broad-leaved and coniferous trees.

SEASON Summer to late autumn.

EDIBLE Not recommended due to its poor flavour and a soggy texture.

SIMILAR SPECIES A very variable species and darker forms look like Bay Bolete while some forms look like Red-cracked Bolete.
