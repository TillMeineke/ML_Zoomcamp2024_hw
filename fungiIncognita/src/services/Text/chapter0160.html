Red-cracked Bolete



Boletus chrysenteron



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A very common Bolete, also known as Xerocomus chrysenteron with a shallowly convex yellow-brown, dry cap which later cracks to reveal a pink-red layer just below the surface. The large, angular yellow pores age olive and bruise blue-green. The cylindrical stem is pale yellow at its apex but flushed red towards the base.


SIZE Cap 5-10 cm, stem 4-8 cm x 10-15 mm.

HABITAT Under broad-leaved trees in woods and gardens.

SEASON Midsummer to late autumn.

EDIBLE Not recommended due to its soggy texture.

SIMILAR SPECIES Yellow-cracked Bolete has no red on the stem or beneath the cracks on the cap. Boletus pruinatus has a red-brown cap with smaller, pale yellow pores which bruise blue.
