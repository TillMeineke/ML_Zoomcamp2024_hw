Shaggy Parasol



Macrolepiota rhacodes



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Frequently confused with Parasol Mushroom, it is smaller, stockier and has a more convex, fleshier cap covered with pale beige scales which bend away from the cap. The white stem bruises red-brown. The cap and stem flesh, together with the cream-coloured gills, turn orange-red when cut. It has a strong, slightly sweet smell.


SIZE Cap 12-18 cm, stem 8-12 cm x 15-20 mm.

HABITAT In grass with trees and near garden compost.

SEASON Midsummer to late autumn.

EDIBLE A minority suffer from digestive upset and skin rash.

SIMILAR SPECIES Variety hortensis is stockier with chestnut-coloured scales. M.konradii is smaller with a smoother brown cap and only very slight flesh reddening.
