Common Morel



Morchella esculenta



Patrick Harding



Patrick Harding


The hollow egg-shaped cap may be globular or more conical and is often not symmetrical. The deeply pitted surface is like honeycomb and the pits vary from pale brown to grey; darker when old. The pale ochre stem is grooved near its base and has a single hollow chamber.


SIZE Cap 3-8 cm across, stem 3-8 cm x 1.5-2.5 cm.

HABITAT On well-drained soil, usually under broad-leaved trees in woodland edges, hedgerows and gardens.

SEASON April to May with a short fruiting season.

EDIBLE Wonderful flavour and texture. Dries well.

SIMILAR SPECIES M. elata has a pointed conical cap with the pits in vertical rows. See Turban Fungus.
