Tawny Grisette



Amanita fulva



Alan Outen


Unlike other common Amanitas, this and the similar, less common, grey-capped Grisette have no ring. Expanding through a white veil, the shiny cap (sticky when moist) is orange-brown with a darker, raised centre and grooved margin. It has white, free gills. The smooth white stem is flushed with the cap colour and narrows towards the apex. The white sack-like volva encloses the stem base.


SIZE Cap 4-8 cm, stem 10-15 cm x 10-15 mm.

HABITAT Broad-leaved woods.

SEASON Midsummer to autumn.

EDIBLE Must be well cooked. Not recommended.

SIMILAR SPECIES Grisette (A. vaginata) is slightly larger and has a grey to grey-brown cap.
