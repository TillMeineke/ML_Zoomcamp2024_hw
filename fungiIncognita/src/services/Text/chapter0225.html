Yellow Brain Fungus



Tremella mesenterica



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This curious fungus is easy to spot after wet weather with its bright yellow-orange colour and very soft, shiny, irregularly folded, gelatinous fruitbody. Originally disc-like it soon becomes more brain-shaped. Attached to pieces of dead wood by a relatively small basal area; when knocked it wobbles like jelly. In dry weather it shrinks, darkens, becomes very hard and is more difficult to find.


SIZE 2-8 cm across.

HABITAT On dead twigs and branches of broad-leaved trees, especially hazel, birch and ash. Also on gorse.

SEASON All year but more frequent in winter.

INEDIBLE Insubstantial, with an odd texture.

SIMILAR SPECIES T. foliacea is date-brown and has leaf-like, undulating lobes.
