Small Bleeding Bell Cap



Mycena sanguinolenta



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The conical cap later flattens but maintains a dark red-brown central umbo. The rest is pinky-brown with darker striations. The adnate pinky-white gills have a red-brown margin. The hollow, long russet stem exudes a pink-red latex when broken.


SIZE Cap 0.5-1.5 cm, stem 4-7 cm x 1 mm.

HABITAT Among moss in both broad-leaved and coniferous woods. Also on heaths.

SEASON Summer to autumn.

EDIBLE Not worthwhile.

SIMILAR SPECIES The larger M.haematopus has a stem which exudes a darker red latex when damaged and grows clustered on rotting wood. The uncommon M.crocata occurs with beech litter and exudes an orange-red juice.
