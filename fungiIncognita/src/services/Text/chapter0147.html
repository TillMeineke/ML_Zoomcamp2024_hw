Rusty Carpet Ink Cap



Coprinus domesticus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Beginners often confuse this with Glistening Ink Cap but the shaggy, rust-coloured vegetative strands at the stem base help to separate them. The bell-shaped, tan-coloured cap has a rusty-brown apex dusted with white granular scales and a grooved margin. The crowded white gills age dark grey with a purple tint before dissolving. The smooth white stem has a swollen base.


SIZE Cap 2-5 cm, stem 4-8 cm x 4-5 mm.

HABITAT On dead wood and stumps of broad-leaved trees.

SEASON Early summer to early autumn.

INEDIBLE Not worth eating.

SIMILAR SPECIES The less common C. xanthothrix also grows from a rust-coloured mat but has pale brown cap scales. Glistening Ink Cap lacks the rust mat.
