Pig’s Ears



Peziza badia



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The genus Peziza contains many medium-sized, thin and fragile species with a cup or saucer-shaped fruitbody and little or no stalk. P. badia is one of the largest and the undulating, irregular fruitbody lacks a stalk. The smooth, inner spore-bearing surface is dark olive-brown; outer is slightly scurfy near the margin and deep red-brown.


SIZE 3-8 cm diameter.

HABITAT Gregarious on moist acid soil, especially on paths and ditch banks.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

POISONOUS Especially if eaten raw.

SIMILAR SPECIES Most other Peziza species are a paler colour on one or both surfaces. See Large Cup Fungus & Straw Cup Fungus.
