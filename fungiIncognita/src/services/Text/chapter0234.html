Pestle Puffball



Calvatia excipuliformis



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This has a long thick stalk region below the spherical apex giving it a pestle or drum-stick shape. The white flesh is protected by a creamy-brown skin covered with tiny spines and granules. These are soon lost revealing the brown, parchment-like inner skin which splits at the apex and peels open, releasing brown spores. The non-fertile, wrinkled stalk region persists after the spores have been shed.


SIZE 8-15 cm high, apex 5-10 cm, stalk 3-5 cm across.

HABITAT In parkland, open broad-leaved woodland and on heaths. Occasional; solitary or in small groups.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

EDIBLE Young specimens are edible.

SIMILAR SPECIES See Common Puffball.
