Spotted Tough Shank



Collybia maculata



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This large, thick-fleshed, initially white species develops rusty-brown spots on its cap, gills and stem. Handling also causes browning. The deeply convex cap matures flat but often misshapen. The white gills are thin, adnexed and very crowded. The tall, broad, fibrous stem continues root-like underground.


SIZE Cap 6-12 cm, stem 8-12 cm x 8-15 mm.

HABITAT Under trees, most commonly in coniferous woods. Also on heaths with bracken.

SEASON Midsummer to late autumn.

INEDIBLE Very tough with a bitter taste.

SIMILAR SPECIES A similar-sized fungus which is also white with pinky-brown blemishes is Tricholoma columbetta but this has sinuate gills and smells of meal.
