Grey Coral Fungus



Clavulina cinerea



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This is very similar to the previous species but slightly larger and the much branched, coral-like fruitbody is grey-brown. The branches are often flattened and the ends are blunt or rounded but not pointed.


SIZE 3-10 cm tall, 2-8 cm wide.

HABITAT In broad-leaved or coniferous woods, usually among leaf litter. Solitary or in small groups.

SEASON Midsummer to late autumn.

EDIBLE Insubstantial and not recommended.

SIMILAR SPECIES Often difficult to separate from Crested Coral Fungus which usually has pointed branch ends. The rare Thelephora palmata has more purple-brown branches and a strong smell of onion.
