Brown Goblet



Coltricia perennis



Patrick Harding



Patrick Harding


Growing on the ground, this species is easy to mistake for a toadstool but it has a thin, tough, corky cap with tubes on its underside. The circular cap is flat or funnel-shaped and velvety, with concentric yellow-brown, rust and grey-brown bands which give it the appearance of tree rings. The pale thin undulating edge often splits. The pores are rusty-brown, angular and decurrent on to the narrow, frequently flattened, velvety brown stem.


SIZE Cap 3-7 cm, stem 2-5 cm x 3-8 mm.

HABITAT On well-drained or burnt ground; often under pine.

SEASON Late summer but old fruitbodies persist.

INEDIBLE However, it is used in floral decorations.

SIMILAR SPECIES Winter Polypore (Polyporus brumalis) lacks the concentric zones and grows on dead wood.
