Shallow-pored Bolete



Suillus bovinus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


As the common name suggests, this has a thin cap; the Latin name refers to the oxen or Jersey cow-like colour of the slimy cap which dries shiny with a paler margin. The large, angular, olive-yellow pores are frequently subdivided and are elongated near the stem where the tubes are slightly decurrent. The smooth cap-coloured, cylindrical stem lacks a ring and often bends at the base.


SIZE Cap 4-12 cm, stem 4-10 cm x 10-15 mm.

HABITAT In grass, moss and heather under Scots pine.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

EDIBLE Poor flavour and rubbery texture.

SIMILAR SPECIES The larger, yellower Larch Bolete grows with larch. Peppery Bolete grows with pine but has a dry, matt cap and a peppery taste.
