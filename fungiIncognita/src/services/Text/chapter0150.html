Fairy Parasol



Coprinus plicatilis



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A very delicate fungus common in short grass and unusual for an Ink Cap in having a cap which finally flattens and shrivels rather than dissolving to a black ink. Initially pale tan, the paper-thin, ribbed, translucent cap turns grey apart from a brown central disc. The thin, spaced, grey-black gills radiate from a collar round the easily broken, tall slender stem.


SIZE Cap 1-2 cm, stem 4-6 cm x 2-3 mm.

HABITAT In short grass and among woodland herbs.

SEASON Spring to early winter.

EDIBLE Far too insubstantial to be worth collecting.

SIMILAR SPECIES A number of related species, e.g. C. leiocephalus, have browner caps and are found beneath trees or in hedgerows.
