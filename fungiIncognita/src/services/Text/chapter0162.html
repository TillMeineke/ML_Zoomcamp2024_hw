Dotted-stemmed Bolete



Boletus erythropus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A large, solid Bolete with yellow flesh in the cap and stem which rapidly turns dark blue on cutting. The fleshy, broadly convex cap is usually dark olive-brown with a velvet surface when young; later smooth and glossy. The small, round yellow pores mature deep orange-red and bruise dark blue. The yellow, club-shaped stem bruises blue and is covered with tiny red-brown dots in the lower region.


SIZE Cap 8-20 cm, stem 6-12 cm x 20-40 mm.

HABITAT Under broad-leaved or coniferous trees, commonly on neutral or acidic soils.

SEASON Early summer to autumn.

POISONOUS Causes stomach upset especially when raw.
