Russet Tough Shank



Collybia dryophila



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Living up to its name with a russet-brown, smooth hollow fibrous stem which is slightly swollen at the base. The thin flat dry cap is initially also russet but fades to pale buff. The very crowded, adnexed gills are creamy-white, sometimes pale yellow. Mushroom-like smell.


SIZE Cap 2-5 cm, stem 3-6 cm x 2-4 mm.

HABITAT Gregarious among leaf litter in broad-leaved woods. Also under bracken on heaths.

SEASON Midsummer to late autumn.

INEDIBLE Tough. Poisonous if eaten raw.

SIMILAR SPECIES The similar-coloured Spindle Shank (C.fusipes) has twisted, flattened stems swollen at the middle and fusing with others at the base. On or near oak and beech stumps.
