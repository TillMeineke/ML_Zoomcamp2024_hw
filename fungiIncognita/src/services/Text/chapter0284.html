Coral Spot



Nectria cinnabarina



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Gregarious on small dead twigs or other wood where the hundreds of minute, cushion-like, dry fruitbodies may almost cover the surface. Most commonly found is the pale orange-pink, non-sexual form which is strictly known as Tubercularia vulgaris. The true sexual form is dark red or red-brown with a bumpy surface. Both forms occasionally grow together.


SIZE 0.5-1.0 mm diameter.

HABITAT Usually on dead branches and twigs of broad-leaved trees and shrubs. Often on wood piles.

SEASON Throughout the year.

INEDIBLE Insubstantial.

SIMILAR SPECIES Orange Jelly produces hundreds of tiny fruitbodies on dead wood but when fresh these are very soft and orange in colour.
