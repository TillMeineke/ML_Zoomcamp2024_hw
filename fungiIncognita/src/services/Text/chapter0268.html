Ascocoryne sarcoides

The soft jelly-like consistency is similar to that of the basidiomycete Jelly Fungi (Jewâ€™s Ear-Jelly Antler Fungus). The tiny purple-pink, spherical fruitbody becomes more like an inverted cone with a smooth flat disc or saucer-shaped top and a very short stalk. The disc edges are wavy and frequently distorted by the proximity of neighbouring fruitbodies. Coryne sarcoides indicates the similar asexual stage.


SIZE 0.5-1.5 cm diameter.

HABITAT Gregarious. Clustered on barkless dead wood of broad-leaved trees, especially beech.

SEASON Most frequent in autumn and winter.

INEDIBLE Insubstantial.

SIMILAR SPECIES A. cylichnium has larger spores. Neobulgaria pura is a pale flesh colour.
