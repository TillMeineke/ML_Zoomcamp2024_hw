Woolly Milk Cap



Lactarius torminosus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This species causes griping pains if eaten without careful preparation. The cap is markedly funnel-shaped when fully grown, sticky at the centre when moist and with an inrolled margin which in young specimens has a texture of lambswool. The flesh-pink cap is marked with concentric, darker pink rings. The gills are pink, crowded and weakly decurrent. They exude a white milk. The pale pink solid stem matures hollow with some surface pits.


SIZE Cap 6-12 cm, stem 6-10 cm x 10-20 mm.

HABITAT With birch on damp peaty soil.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

POISONOUS Parboiled it is eatable but not edible.

SIMILAR SPECIES L.pubescens has a paler, non-zoned cap. Saffron Milk Cap has orange gills and milk.
