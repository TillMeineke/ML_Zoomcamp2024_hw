Parasitic Bolete



Boletus parasiticus



Patrick Harding



Patrick Harding


This small, infrequent Bolete is instantly recognisable as it is always found attached to Common Earth Ball which it parasitises. The pale, olive-brown dry cap is initially velvety but cracks on maturity. The margin remains inrolled until old. The large, angular, golden-yellow pores age red-brown. The yellow-brown stem often narrows at its base and curves from the underside of its earthball host. Also known as Xerocomus parasiticus.


SIZE Cap 2-5 cm, stem 3-6 cm x 8-12 mm.

HABITAT Singly or in small tufts on Common Earthball.

SEASON Autumn.

EDIBLE Not recommended as it is quite rare.

SIMILAR SPECIES None; the only parasitic Bolete.
