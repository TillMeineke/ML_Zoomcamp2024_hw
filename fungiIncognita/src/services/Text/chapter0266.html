Green Wood Cup



Chlorosplenium aeruginascens



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Wood which has been stained blue-green by the action of this fungus is commonly found; the fruitbodies are rarer. Initially goblet-shaped with a short stalk, the top becomes saucer-shaped and later irregularly wavy. The smooth upper surface is bright blue-green; the outer surface and stalk start paler but mature blue-green. It is soft in texture. Also known as Chlorociboria aeruginascens.


SIZE 0.5-1.0 cm diameter.

HABITAT On dead wood of broad-leaved trees e.g. oak.

SEASON From spring to autumn.

INEDIBLE The stained wood is part of the intricate patterns employed in Tunbridge ware.

SIMILAR SPECIES None.
