Verdigris Toadstool



Stropharia aeruginosa



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Fresh specimens have slimy, shiny caps; young ones are dark, blue-green and bell-shaped, older ones more convex and grass-green. The cap margin is peppered with small white scales. Old caps lack slime and scales, fading to pale tan. The crowded, sinuate gills start pale grey but age purple-brown with a white margin. The stem is white above the transient ring (coloured by the purple-black spores); it is pale blue-green with white scales below (as cap margin).


SIZE Cap 2-6 cm, stem 4-8 cm x 5-10 mm.

HABITAT Among grass in woods and in pasture.

SEASON Midsummer to autumn.

POISONOUS Fortunately the colour is offputting.

SIMILAR SPECIES Specimens with smooth cap and stem are sometimes placed under S.caerulea.
