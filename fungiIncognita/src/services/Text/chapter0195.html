Hedgehog Fungus



Hydnum repandum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This species has spines (stalactite-like projections) from the cap in place of gills. It has a medium-sized, fleshy, creamy-buff cap with an inrolled, often wavy or irregular margin. Neighbouring caps may coalesce. The spines are paler than the cap, crowded, brittle and continuing on to the top of the short stocky stem which is often not centrally attached.


SIZE Cap 6-14 cm, stem 4-6 cm x 15-25 mm.

HABITAT Under both broad-leaved (especially beech) and coniferous trees; often in troops.

SEASON Autumn.

EDIBLE Slightly bitter when raw but excellent after cooking, with a firm texture.

SIMILAR SPECIES H. rufescens is smaller and more orange-red.
