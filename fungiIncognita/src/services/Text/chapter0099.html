Saffron Milk Cap



Lactarius deliciosus



Patrick Harding



Patrick Harding


This beautiful fungus is infrequent in Britain. Its large funnel-shaped cap has an inrolled margin when young and is slightly sticky. It is cream to pinky-orange with concentric bands of deep orange and faint green patches when old. The bright orange gills are shortly decurrent, crowded and produce a carrot-coloured milk. The stocky hollow stem is marked on the surface with orange pits near its base.


SIZE Cap 5-15 cm, stem 4-8 cm x 15-20 mm.

HABITAT Under pine trees.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

EDIBLE Best blanched before being fried.

SIMILAR SPECIES The commoner Spruce Milk Cap has a smooth stem. L.salmonicolor has a less zoned cap.
