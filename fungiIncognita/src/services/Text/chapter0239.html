Membranous Puffball



Vascellum pratense



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


One of the commonest puffballs in short grass, it is broadly pear-shaped and flattened at the apex. It is often wider than it is tall and the base is barely narrower. When young the white skin is granular but the granules wash off in the rain leaving a smooth, pale brown surface. Inside, the olive-brown spore mass is clearly separated from the white lower non-fertile region by a membrane. The spores escape through a large apical hole.


SIZE 2-5 cm diameter, 2-4 cm high.

HABITAT Gregarious in lawns, heaths and near the sea.

SEASON Summer to autumn. Lower region persists.

EDIBLE Only edible when the flesh is white.

SIMILAR SPECIES The distinct membrane between the fertile and non-fertile region prevents confusion.
