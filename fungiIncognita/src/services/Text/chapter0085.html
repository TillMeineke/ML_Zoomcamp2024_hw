The Sickener



Russula emetica



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The best-known of the Russulas because of its reputation for causing sickness. The convex cap, which soon flattens, is thin-fleshed and easily broken. It is shiny and sticky when wet with easily peelable skin which is a brilliant cherry-red with some paler areas. The creamy-white adnexed gills are moderately spaced. The spongy white stem is swollen at the base. It has a slightly fruity smell.


SIZE Cap 5-10 cm, stem 4-8 cm x 15-20 mm.

HABITAT Under conifers on moist acid soil, often with moss.

SEASON Midsummer to autumn.

POISONOUS The acrid flesh causes vomiting.

SIMILAR SPECIES Beechwood Sickener.
