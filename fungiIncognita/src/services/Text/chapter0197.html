Ear-pick Fungus



Auriscalpium vulgare



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A curious little fungus growing on old pine cones. The thin dark brown cap is shaped like an ear and the convex surface is coarsely velvety and often channelled to the laterally attached stem. In place of gills there are tiny (2-3 mm), grey-brown spines which hang down like stalactites. The slender, cap-coloured stem has a bristly surface.


SIZE Cap 1-2 cm, stem 2-6 cm x 1-2 mm.

HABITAT Singly or in small groups on decaying (often buried) cones of pine and occasionally spruce.

SEASON Mostly autumn but appearing all year.

INEDIBLE Insubstantial and tough.

SIMILAR SPECIES Two other common fungi fruit on cones - Strobilurus esculentus (spring) and Baeospora myosura (autumn) but these have gills, not spines.
