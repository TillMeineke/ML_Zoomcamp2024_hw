Coconut-scented Milk Cap



Lactarius glyciosmus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A small Milk Cap easily recognised by its coconut smell. The cap colour varies from grey-lilac to pinky-brown. It has a dry, downy texture, thin flesh and an incurled margin when young. The crowded decurrent gills are flesh-coloured and produce small amounts of white milk when damaged. The stem is paler than the cap, becoming hollow and fragile with age.


SIZE Cap 2-5 cm, stem 3-6 cm x 4-8 mm.

HABITAT Under broad-leaved trees, mostly with birch.

SEASON Late summer to late autumn.

EDIBLE Used for flavouring but has a fairly hot taste.

SIMILAR SPECIES The larger Grey Milk Cap (L.vietus) has a sticky cap and a milk which dries grey on the gills; these bruise pale brown.
