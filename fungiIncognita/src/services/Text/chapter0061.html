Clustered Brown Cap



Lyophyllum decastes



Patrick Harding



Patrick Harding


The fruitbodies are in crowded clusters. The almost hemispherical young caps age more convex with a broad central umbo where the grey to hazel-brown colour is darkest. The margin is wavy. The gills are adnate and a dirty white colour. The grey-white stem is usually flattened, bent and twisted, fusing with several others.


SIZE Cap 6-10 cm, stem 5-10 cm x 10-18 mm.

HABITAT Near stumps or from buried wood in woodland, gardens, parks and even pavements.

SEASON Midsummer to autumn.

EDIBLE Not highly rated. Beware highly poisonous lookalike Livid Entoloma.

SIMILAR SPECIES L.connatum, is white with shortly decurrent gills. Beware the poisonous Livid Entoloma.
