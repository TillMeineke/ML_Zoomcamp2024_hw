Common Ink Cap



Coprinus atramentarius



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The grey to fawn cap is initially egg-shaped, later bell-shaped. The smooth surface breaks into a few tiny scales at the apex; the margin is often wavy and split. The ascending gills mature from white to grey and finally black when they dissolve into an inky fluid. It has a smooth, hollow white stem with an ill-defined ring zone.


SIZE Cap 4-8 cm, stem 5-15 cm x 10-15 mm.

HABITAT Gregarious from tree stumps or in grass, gardens, pavements - wherever buried wood is present.

SEASON Spring to early winter.

POISONOUS It reacts with alcohol in the bloodstream causing nausea and hot flushes.

SIMILAR SPECIES The woodland C. acuminatus is smaller.
