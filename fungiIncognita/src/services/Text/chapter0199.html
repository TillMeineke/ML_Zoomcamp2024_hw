Earth Fan



Thelephora terrestris



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


With no cap, stem or gills, its fruitbody consists of flattened, soft, grey-brown, fan-shaped lobes which frequently fuse to give a rosette appearance. The lobes are either semi-erect in a shallow bowl-shape or flat and encrusting twigs and plant stems. The upper surface is radially zoned and felty with a paler, irregularly fringed margin. The smooth, lower spore-bearing surface is cinnamon-brown and wrinkled.


SIZE In patches, 3-20 cm across.

HABITAT On soil or needle litter under conifers; also under broad-leaved trees and on heaths.

SEASON Summer to late autumn.

INEDIBLE Insubstantial

SIMILAR SPECIES Hairy Stereum grows on wood.
