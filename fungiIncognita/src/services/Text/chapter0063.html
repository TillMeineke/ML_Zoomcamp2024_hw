Plums and custard



Tricholomopsis rutilans



G. Redeuilh



G. Redeuilh


The name refers to the colour of the cap and gills. The large, flat cap is covered with fine red-purple scales, less dense near the incurved margins where the background yellow shows through. The apricot-yellow gills are weakly sinuate and crowded. The scaly, cap-coloured stem often curves. It has a musty smell.


SIZE Cap 4-12 cm, stem 4-10 cm x 10-25 mm.

HABITAT On old conifer stumps, mostly with pine.

SEASON Autumn.

INEDIBLE Not poisonous but not edible despite its name.

SIMILAR SPECIES The smaller, yellower T.decora also grows on conifer stumps.
