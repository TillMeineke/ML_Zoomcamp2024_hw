Brown Birch Bolete



Leccinum scabrum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Commoner than the preceding species with a dull, grey-brown smaller, spongy-textured cap and no overhanging margin. The tubes round the stem are much shorter than elsewhere. The tiny grey pores bruise pale brown. The cylindrical white stem is peppered with tiny brown-black scales. The white, soft flesh does not change colour on cutting or cooking.


SIZE Cap 5-10 cm, stem 8-15 cm x 20-30 mm.

HABITAT Under birch; locally abundant.

SEASON Early summer to autumn.

EDIBLE Soggy. Only young caps are worth eating.

SIMILAR SPECIES Easily confused with Orange Birch Bolete; this is larger, firmer and has flesh that blues on cutting. L. roseofractum has a dark brown cap and flesh that turns dark pink on cutting.
