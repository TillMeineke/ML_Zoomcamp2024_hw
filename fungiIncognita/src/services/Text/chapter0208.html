Root Fomes



Heterobasidion annosum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A serious parasite of conifers, especially spruce, the fruitbodies are formed from the lower trunk/upper root zone where the brackets are often partly hidden by moss and grass. It is crust-like but also produces small, irregular, elongated lobes. These are dark brown to black, with a hard lumpy surface and a wavy margin showing the white of the underside which is covered with small angular pores. If cut open older fruitbodies show annual layers of tubes.


SIZE Up to 15 cm across, 8 cm broad and 4 cm thick.

HABITAT With conifers; mostly at the base of spruce trees.

SEASON Throughout the year as it is perennial.

INEDIBLE Very hard.

SIMILAR SPECIES None in the same habitat.
