Mosaic Puffball



Calvatia utriformis



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This puffball is the size of a large tennis ball; with a flattened top and a two-layered skin. The rough white outer skin cracks into pyramid-shaped warts but these soon disappear to reveal a white inner skin patterned with a honeycomb-like mosaic. Later smooth and brown, when a hole appears and the edges spread back to release the brown spores. The leathery non-fertile base persists like a thick saucer.


SIZE 5-15 cm diameter.

HABITAT In small groups on unimproved upland pasture. More frequent in the north.

SEASON Summer to autumn. Brown basal region persists.

EDIBLE The inner flesh must be still white.

SIMILAR SPECIES Small specimens of Giant Puffball have a single smooth skin and no infertile base.
