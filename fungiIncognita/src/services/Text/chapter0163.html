Lurid Bolete



Boletus luridus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The yellow-brown fleshy cap bears tubes with orange-red pores which, like the cap, stem and cut flesh, bruise blue-green. The broad stem is yellowish at the top but most of the rest is covered by a purple-red, longitudinally elongated net.


SIZE Cap 8-14 cm, stem 5-12 cm x 15-40 mm.

HABITAT Under beech, oak or lime on chalky soils.

SEASON Midsummer to autumn.

POISONOUS Causes digestive upset and produces unpleasant symptoms with alcohol.
