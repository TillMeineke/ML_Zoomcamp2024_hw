Yellow-brown Tricholoma



Tricholoma fulvum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The orange-brown cap flattens but for a broad, darker, central umbo and a faintly ribbed margin. The surface is smooth, shiny and sticky when young. The broad, sinuate yellow-brown gills often have darker spots. The stem apex is paler than the cap but darker at the stem base and covered with red-brown fibres. It is sticky when moist and has an unpleasant mealy smell.


SIZE Cap 5-10 cm, stem 7-11 cm x 10-15 mm.

HABITAT On wet, acidic soils mostly under birch. Occasionally with conifers.

SEASON Autumn.

EDIBLE Poor. Can be confused with other brown species.

SIMILAR SPECIES T.ustale with red-staining gills. T.populinum has white gills and grows under aspen and poplar.
