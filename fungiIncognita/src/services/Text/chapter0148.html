Glistening Ink Cap



Coprinus micaceus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


More easily identified from young specimens when the ochre-brown, bell-shaped caps are dusted with similar-coloured, glistening, mica-like grains. The older caps lose these and the grooved margin often splits and turns up. The crowded pale gills age brown then black before dissolving into a black fluid. The white stem browns at its base.


SIZE Cap 2-4 cm, stem 5-8 cm x 2-4 mm.

HABITAT In dense clusters on broad-leaved stumps and logs. Also from dead roots.

SEASON Throughout the year.

EDIBLE Hardly worthwhile; poor flavour and texture.

SIMILAR SPECIES Frequently confused with Rusty Carpet Ink Cap which has white grains and grows from a rust-coloured mat.
