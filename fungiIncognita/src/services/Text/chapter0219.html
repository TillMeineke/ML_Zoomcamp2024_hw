Giant Polypore



Meripilus giganteus



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Produces clusters of overlapping brackets up to a metre across. Each fan-shaped lobe arises from a common bulbous base. Tan-brown, radially wrinkled and faintly zoned with fine, darker brown fibres. The undulating thin margin often divides into smaller sub-brackets. The small-pored cream underside blackens on handling as does the flesh.


SIZE Lobes 10-35 cm across, 1-3 cm thick.

HABITAT On stumps, roots or near the base of living broad-leaved trees; mostly on beech.

SEASON Summer to early autumn. Soon rotting.

EDIBLE Slow-cooked young brackets are edible but can cause gastric upset in some people.
