Common Crumble Cap



Psathyrella candolleana



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Only old caps show the dark gill and spore colour, so they are frequently confused with white and pink-spored species. This one has a thin-fleshed, ochre-yellow cap which soon flattens and dries pale cream, especially near the margin which frequently splits and bears dark veil remnants. The gills are crowded, adnexed and initially white then lilac-grey before turning dark brown. The cap does not dissolve. The stem is thin, white and brittle.


SIZE Cap 3-6 cm, stem 5-8 cm x 3-5 mm.

HABITAT In tufts near broad-leaved tree stumps.

SEASON Early summer to autumn.

EDIBLE Said to be edible if cooked but not worthwhile.

SIMILAR SPECIES There are many Crumble Caps; most grow in small groups on soil or among grass.
