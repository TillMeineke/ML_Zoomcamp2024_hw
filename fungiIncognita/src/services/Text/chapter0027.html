Clouded Agaric



Clitocybe nebularis



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


A large fungus often growing in rings. The thick fleshy convex cap matures flat, often with a depressed centre and inrolled edge. It is grey-brown, darker at the centre and frequently with a white bloom. The gills are crowded, cream-coloured, adnate to slightly decurrent. The stem is paler, and thick with a swollen base. It smells strongly of turnip.


SIZE Cap 8-20 cm, stem 5-12 cm x 20-30 mm.

HABITAT In litter of broad-leaved and coniferous woods.

SEASON Autumn to early winter.

EDIBLE Can cause digestive upset so best avoided.

SIMILAR SPECIES It can be confused with Wood Blewit which also has pale-pink spores and an inrolled cap edge but with sinuate, initially lilac-coloured gills and a fruity smell.
