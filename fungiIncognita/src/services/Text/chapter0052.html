Velvet Shank



Flammulina velutipes



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This species continues fruiting through the winter. The medium-sized caps soon flatten and are a distinctive orange-brown, darker at the centre, slightly lined at the margin. The surface is shiny and sticky. The adnate, broad spaced white gills mature pale yellow. The stem is tough, velvety, cap-coloured at the apex and dark brown at the base.


SIZE Cap 2-7 cm, stem 3-10 cm x 3-8 mm.

HABITAT Clustered on dead wood (stumps, logs and standing timber) of broad-leaved trees, typically on elm.

SEASON Autumn to spring.

EDIBLE Remove the slimy layer and tough stem.

SIMILAR SPECIES Confusable with other tufted wood rotters such as Brown Stew Fungus and Sulphur Tuft but these have brown spores and a ringed stem.
