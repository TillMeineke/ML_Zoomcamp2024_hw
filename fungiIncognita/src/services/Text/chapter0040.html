Bonnet Bell Cap



Mycena galericulata



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


The bell-shaped cap is light brown to grey, paler at the margin which is furrowed and later upturned. It flattens and wrinkles with age but retains a central umbo. The gills are pale grey with a flesh tint, adnate and spaced with cross veins. The stem is smooth, often curved, the same colour as the cap, with white hairs at its base. It smells of meal.


SIZE Cap 2-6 cm, stem 3-8 cm x 2-5 mm.

HABITAT Clustered on broad-leaved stumps and fallen logs.

SEASON Most of the year.

EDIBLE Insubstantial and not worth eating.

SIMILAR SPECIES Stump Bell Cap is more densely tufted, has an ammonia-like smell and is associated with coniferous trees.
