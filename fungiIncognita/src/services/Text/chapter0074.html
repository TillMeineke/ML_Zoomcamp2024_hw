Common Yellow Russula



Russula ochroleuca



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


Russulas are unusual in that they can be readily crumbled. In this, the commonest of the yellow species, the convex cap soon flattens and may become concave. The matt, ochre-yellow, peelable skin matures with a faintly grooved edge. The pale cream gills are neat, adnate and quite crowded. The white cylindrical stem greys with age. The taste is acrid.


SIZE Cap 4-10 cm, stem 4-8 cm x 10-20 mm.

HABITAT In both broad-leaved and coniferous woods.

SEASON Late summer to early winter.
