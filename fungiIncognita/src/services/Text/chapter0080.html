Birch Russula



Russula betularum



Alan Outen



Alan Outen


This small, thin-fleshed Russula is always found near birch. The convex to flat cap is pale red-pink with areas of yellow-buff. The furrowed margin is frequently warty. The entire skin can be peeled. The gills are adnexed, white and with a slightly toothed edge. The white stem is often longer than the cap diameter. The smell is faintly fruity.


SIZE Cap 2-6 cm, stem 3-7 cm x 5-8 mm

HABITAT Under birch; often in moss on damp ground.

SEASON Late summer to autumn.

INEDIBLE Very acrid taste.

SIMILAR SPECIES Other pink-red species are not restricted to birch and include Fragile Russula and Bare-toothed Russula.
